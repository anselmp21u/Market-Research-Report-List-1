<p><p>The chlorine-resistant fiber market can be categorized based on production methods: dry spinning and wet spinning. </p><p>In the dry spinning method, fibers are created by dissolving polymers in a solvent and then evaporating the solvent, resulting in durable fibers suitable for swimwear and sports apparel. </p><p>Wet spinning involves dissolving polymers in a chemical solution, followed by extrusion into a coagulating bath, producing fibers with enhanced strength and chemical resistance. Both methods cater to the growing demand for chlorine-resistant textiles.</p></p>
